My research projects

Here is a list of my current and past research projects related to DNA data storage. You can click on the links to access the project websites or reports.

  • DNA Data Storage and Retrieval using Nanopore Sequencing: This is my current project at NUS, funded by the NRF. The goal of this project is to develop a novel method for retrieving data from DNA using nanopore sequencing, which is a fast and portable technology that can read single molecules of DNA. The project involves designing and testing new encoding and decoding algorithms, as well as optimizing the synthesis and sequencing processes.
  • Deep Neural Network for Early Detection of Acute Decompensated Heart Failure: A Cutting-Edge Deep Neural Network Framework for the Prediction of Acute Decompensated Heart Failure: A Collaborative Project with All India Institute of Medical Sciences
  • Deep Learning for Cardiovascular Disease: A Novel Deep Learning Method for the Detection and Management of Cardiovascular Disease: A Collaboration with the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign
  • Deep Neural Network for Early Detection of Pancreatic Cancer: An Innovative Deep Neural Network Methodology for the Preemptive and Early Identification of Pancreatic Cancer: A Collaboration with Henry Ford Health System
